# Moon Phase from Julian Date

Trace the Moon's phase from Julian Date.  
Derive mean elongation *D* first.  
*D* spans the angle between Sun and Moon, viewed from Earth.  
Ephemeris polynomials—calibrated to J2000.0 epoch—yield *D*.  

Take the phase angle as the fractional part of *D*/360.  
0° marks new Moon.  
180° crowns full Moon.  

Compute illuminated fraction *k*: *(1 - cos(*D*))/2*.  
Anomaly corrections sharpen percentage illumination to sub-percent precision.
