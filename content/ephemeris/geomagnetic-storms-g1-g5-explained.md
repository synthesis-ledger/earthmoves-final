## The G1-G5 Geomagnetic Storm Scale

NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center crafted the G-scale. It converts solar wind clashes with Earth's magnetosphere into five levels. Each ties to a Kp threshold. Kp tracks global geomagnetic unrest from 0 to 9. Thirteen ground magnetometers feed the data.

Kp=5- launches G1 storms. Coronal mass ejections or fast solar streams compress the dayside magnetosphere. Plasma floods the nightside.

G1 (Kp=5-) ignites substorms. Auroras flare poleward of 60° geomagnetic latitude—in northern Scandinavia, Alaska. G2 (Kp=6.0) pushes to 55°—southern Canada, northern Scotland. Currents strain power lines.

G3 (Kp=7.0) drops auroras to 50°—New York, central Europe. Voltage dips demand fixes in long lines.

G4 (Kp=8.0) shoves ovals to 45°—Alabama, northern Italy. Voltage swings wild. Satellites drag low.

G5 (Kp=9.0) twists the magnetosphere. Auroras crown Hawaii, India. Currents surge hundreds of amperes through grids, pipelines. Electrons ravage spacecraft.
