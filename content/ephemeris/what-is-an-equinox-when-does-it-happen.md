## What Is an Equinox and When Does It Happen?

An equinox occurs when the Sun's centre crosses the celestial equator—this imaginary line projects Earth's equator onto the dome of stars. These crossings are named the vernal equinox in March (as the Sun moves northward) and the autumnal equinox in September (southward). North of the equator, spring stirs after the vernal equinox; fall follows the autumnal.
