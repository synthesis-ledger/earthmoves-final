## Satellite Data Streams: L1 Solar Wind and GOES X-Rays

DSCOVR sits at L1, 1.496 million km from Earth. It watches solar wind 25–60 minutes before impact. Faraday cup gauges electron flux for proton speed (300–900 km/s), density (0.5–50 p/cm³). Magnetometers track IMF Bt (1–20 nT), By, Bz (±15 nT). Data refreshes every 60 seconds. Plots hold 1-minute resolution at swpc.noaa.gov/products/real-time-solar-wind.

ACE, from 1997, adds SWEPAM, MAG data. Archive spans 27 years: mean speed 430 km/s, density 5.6 p/cm³, Bz 2.5 nT rms. Bz south of -5 nT for 3+ hours at >400 km/s revs auroral electrojets. AE index leaps past 1000 nT.

M1.0 peaks in minutes, blacks radio to 30 MHz. Older SXI imagers film full disk at 1–12 minutes, spots active regions pre-CME.

Models propagate L1 data. WSA-ENLIL simulates heliosphere plasma, forecasts shocks within 1-hour error. GOES SUVI images 6 EUV bands since -16. CMEs at 195 Å exceed 10¹⁶ g. Fast 700 km/s wind compresses magnetosphere. Aurora ovals widen 10° latitude per Kp.
