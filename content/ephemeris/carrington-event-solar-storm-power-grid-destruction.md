# Carrington Event: The Solar Storm That Could Harm the Power Grid and Disrupt Modern Civilisation

Our life-giving Sun is continously sending exploding burst of particles into space. These solar flares hits Earth from time to time, and it is what is making aurora borealis or northern lights at the North and South Pole areas. A Carrington-level solar storm is a huge explosion on the Sun that coincidentally hits Earth with an enormous force in the form of charged particles. When it hits Earth this storm will not only create spectacular aurora all the way down to Meditterean, it can also induce electricity in numerous hardware. The induced currents can overwhelm high-voltage transformers. This again will cause grids to fail across the most of the Northern and Southern hemisphere. Cascading blackouts. Satellites fail. GPS vanishes for weeks. Planes ground. Trade and digital communication halts.

## Reliving the 1859 Carrington Event

{{image:/ephemeris/carrington-event-solar-storm-power-grid-destruction-inline-2-2.webp|Telegraph lines igniting during the original 1859 Carrington solar storm|Generated by Earth Moves AI}}

Richard Carrington peered through his Kew Observatory refractor on 1 September 1859. He observed a white-light complex that blazed for 5 minutes across 4° of the solar disk. Seventeen hours later, the coronal mass ejection struck Earth.

Auroras was observed to 18°N in Hawaii and Colombia. Skies glowed "bright as day" from Caribbean to Queensland. Boston telegraph wires almost caught fire and operators got shock treatment at 1000 volts. Fires sparked in Pittsburgh and Dusseldorf.

Colaba, India, measured 5000 nanoTesla/min—five times more than the later 1989 Quebec storm. Carrington tied the solar flare to aurora. He measured that the plasma hurtled at 2000 km/s. In 1859 there were almost no electrical equipment installed on Earth, there were no sattellites, and no digital services to be harmed.

A Carrington level ejection happens quite often on the Sun, but it also needs to hit Earth, and Earth is a small target, thats why there has been few Carrington events the last hundred years.

Obviously for every year humankind and our current civilisation is more and more vulnerable since we are getting more dependent on sattellites, digital devices and the electric grid.

## Why Another Carrington-Level Storm Is Inevitable

Solar cycles of flare activity pulse every 11 years. We are currently in a cycle which had a low in 2019 and that topped July 2025. Ice-cores show beryllium-10 peaks which is a sign of a major hit on Earth, that a Carrington 1859-scale are recorded in AD 717, 1770, 1839. Today, experts call a Carrington event is probable every 150 years.

Luckily we now monitor the solar space weather with the SOHO and SDO projects. Their coronagraphs spot coronal mass ejections and can provide a warning of 12-48 hours of Earth-directed ones.

What can you do if an alarm goes off and you have 12 hours to prepare?

- Top up everything: charge phones, power banks, laptops, rechargeable lights, and fill all available water containers in case pumps fail.
- Turn off and unplug non‑essential electronics (PCs, TVs, extra gear) to reduce surge risk if the grid gets hit.
- Keep at least one simple battery or hand‑crank radio, plus spare batteries, ready so you can still get information if internet and mobile networks go down.
- Prepp your home and family as you would do for an extreme weather event.
