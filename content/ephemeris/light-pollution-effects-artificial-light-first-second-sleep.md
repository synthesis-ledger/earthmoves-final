# Light Pollution Effects: How Artificial Light Ended First & Second Sleep

Thomas Edison's incandescent bulb, commercialised in 1879, ignited artificial light. This glow crushed our ancient bimodal sleep. First sleep fell from dusk to about 1 a.m. A wakeful midnight paused 1–2 hours. Moonlight silvered the skin. Stars pierced the dark. Second sleep carried us to dawn.

Now one solid block serves industrial clocks. The glare veils the Milky Way from 80% of humanity. In Europe and North America, 99% lose it. We drift, unmoored from the night sky. Circadian rhythms, etched by natural dark, fracture.
