Summer bloated daytime hours to 4,200 seconds in Thebes' heat. Night hours swelled past 6,000. Winter flipped it. Day shrank under 3,600 seconds. Nights gripped the dark.

## Babylonian Base-60: The Arithmetic Anchor

Babylonians forged base-60 around 2000 BCE. Sixty divides richly: by 1 through 6, 10, 12, 15, 20, 30. Decimal lags. Circles split to 360 degrees—or 6,000 arc minutes.
