# Earth Moves Watch: Live NOAA SWPC Space Weather Integration Explained

Earth Moves Watch pulls live space weather data every 10 seconds from three NOAA SWPC endpoints. Real-Time Solar Wind (RTSW) feed tracks solar wind speed in km/s and Bz in nT. Kp geomagnetic index feed follows disturbances. GOES satellite X-ray flux feed captures solar bursts.

## Live Space Weather from NOAA SWPC

The Sun hurls plasma and fields toward us. Earth Moves Watch [*etches them live*](https://earth-moves.app/earth-moves-watch). JavaScript in your browser queries NOAA SWPC APIs every 10 seconds. Dials pulse. Raw data turns visceral.

DSCOVR sits at L1, 1.5 million km sunward. Solar wind hits it 45–60 minutes before Earth. Speeds clock 300–800 km/s. Bz—the southward field in nanotesla—sets the sapphire Earth's coronal glow. Below -10 nT, storms brew. Auroras ignite.

Kp averages three-hour readings from 13 magnetometers worldwide. It scales geomagnetic unrest from 0 to 9. Needles climb as Earth's field quivers. Satellites falter. Power grids hum uneasily.

GOES satellites scan X-ray flux in real time. Flares spike: C-class hums, M-class bites, X-class roars. Ionized air scatters radio signals. Ham operators fall silent.
